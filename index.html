Gym-specific notes
Starting weights: Begin at 40–50% of what you think your max is. Form first, always.
Progression rule: Add weight only when you hit the top of the rep range with 2 reps to spare for 2 consecutive sessions.
Rest between sets: 90 sec for isolation, 2–3 min for compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows).
At 43: Prioritise full range of motion and controlled tempo over heavy load. Injuries set you back weeks.
Zone 2 cardio — Tue & Sat
What it is: Steady-state effort at 120–140 bpm — conversational pace. You can speak in full sentences.
Why: Best intervention for visceral fat reduction. Also builds mitochondria for long-term energy.
Options: Brisk walk, cycle, swim, elliptical, stair climbing, Surya Namaskar flow (12+ rounds non-stop)
Duration: 35–45 min Tuesday, 45–60 min Saturday

Weeks 1–4 · Foundation
Build the movement pattern and consistency
Lower volumes, master form. 3 sets per exercise. Focus on hitting all 5 training days without missing. Home: incline push-ups, chair squats, towel rows. Gym: light weights on all compounds. Cardio: 30–35 min walks. Goal: 7 sessions/week with ≥70% habit score.
Weeks 5–8 · Build strength
Progressive overload begins, volume increases
4 sets per compound exercise. Home: progress to standard push-ups, full squats, table rows. Gym: add 1–2 kg per week on lifts where form is solid. Cardio: extend to 40–45 min. Expect waist to start dropping. Weigh yourself Sunday mornings — should see 0.3–0.4 kg/week fat trend.
Weeks 9–12 · Intensity
Challenging variants, heavier loads, visible changes
Home: Bulgarian split squats, archer push-ups, feet-elevated table rows. Gym: comfortable with compound lifts, working at 70–75% of max. Cardio: add 1 HIIT session (20 min) replacing one Zone 2 per week if feeling strong. By week 12: waist should be 87–89 cm, body fat ~22%, energy and sleep noticeably improved.
Months 4–6 · Recomposition deepens
Cycle to a new 12-week block with higher baseline
Repeat the 12-week cycle but starting from your stronger baseline. Home users can graduate to minimal equipment. Gym users increase loads meaningfully. By month 6: ~22% body fat, waist ~88 cm, visibly more muscular.
Months 7–12 · Long-term goal
17–19% body fat, waist ≤85 cm, lean mass ≥52 kg
These numbers represent genuinely excellent health for a 44-year-old Indian man. Cardiovascular risk profile normalises, visceral fat in healthy range, functional strength for decades ahead. Maintenance from here requires only 3 strength sessions + 2 cardio per week.

Non-negotiable rules
Protein at every meal — prevents muscle loss during deficit
Salad first — large raw salad before lunch and dinner. Controls glucose spike and portion size.
Post-meal 10-min walk — blunts glucose spike. Directly reduces visceral fat over time.
Finish eating by 8 pm — 12-hour fast every night
No liquid calories — chai max 1 tsp jaggery, no juices, no soft drinks
Zero maida and white rice at dinner — these spike insulin before sleep, directly feeding visceral fat
Indian-specific anti-inflammatory spices
Haldi (turmeric) — curcumin reduces insulin resistance. Use daily in cooking or warm milk.
Jeera (cumin) — improves digestion and reduces bloating. Toast and add to dal/raita.
Methi (fenugreek) — proven to lower post-meal glucose. Soak seeds overnight, eat in morning.
Hing (asafoetida) — reduces gas, improves absorption of dal protein.
Dalchini (cinnamon) — improves insulin sensitivity. Add to chai or oats.
Adrak (ginger) — anti-inflammatory, improves digestion. Raw in water or cooking.

Supplements & longevity stack
Evidence-based · dose-appropriate for Indian men 40+ · consult your doctor before starting
Priority 1 — essential (start immediately)
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–4,000 IU D3 + 100 mcg K2 (MK-7) daily
South Asian men have 70–80% prevalence of deficiency. Critical for immunity, testosterone, bone density.
Plant protein powder
1–2 scoops (~20–40g protein) per day
Pea or soy — helps hit 125g protein without eggs. Use in oats, shakes, or mixed into dal.
Creatine monohydrate
3–5g daily, any time
Most researched supplement. Builds strength, protects brain and bones. Especially effective at 40+. No cycling needed.
Priority 2 — highly recommended
Magnesium glycinate
200–400 mg at night
60–70% of Indians are deficient. Improves sleep quality, muscle recovery, reduces anxiety and cortisol.
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
300–600 mg daily with food
Lowers cortisol, improves body composition and testosterone in men 40+. Indian herb — well-studied.
Omega-3 (algae-based)
1–2g EPA+DHA daily
Vegetarian source. Reduces visceral fat inflammation, improves heart health and brain function.
Priority 3 — consider after 3 months
Zinc
15–25 mg daily with food
Important for testosterone production. Vegetarian diets are often low in bioavailable zinc.
Vitamin B12
500–1000 mcg daily or weekly high dose
Vegetarians are commonly deficient. Critical for nerve health, energy, and red blood cells.
Coenzyme Q10
100–200 mg daily
Mitochondrial energy production declines after 40. Particularly relevant if feeling chronically fatigued.
Blood tests to get done first
HbA1c — screens for pre-diabetes. Very common in Indian men with visceral fat.
Fasting lipid panel — LDL, HDL, triglycerides, total cholesterol
25-OH Vitamin D — baseline before supplementing
Testosterone (total + free) — common to be low at 43 with high visceral fat
TSH (thyroid) — undiagnosed hypothyroidism explains stubborn fat in many people
Serum B12 + ferritin — vegetarian deficiencies that drain energy
Get these done before you start to establish a true baseline. Re-test at 6 months.
